# 8. Analogous Inspiration

## Attribution

Adapted for this agentic workflow from the IDEO.org Design Kit method
"Analogous Inspiration."

## Purpose

Find patterns from adjacent contexts that may reframe the problem without
turning those patterns into premature solutions.

## Inputs

- Active challenge frame
- Constraint and landscape facts
- Resource-flow and power-map claims
- Prior exercise takeaways

## Procedure

1. List the activities, behaviors, interactions, or emotions the team needs to
   understand more deeply.
2. For each item, identify other settings where a similar behavior or mechanism
   appears in a different context.
3. Vote on which analogous settings are most promising and arrange observation
   visits or desk research.
4. Observe the analogous setting with a clear learning goal while staying open
   to unexpected patterns.
5. Translate useful patterns back into the original challenge as questions,
   hypotheses, or design principles, not as copied solutions.

## Tasks

1. Identify the underlying behavior, constraint, or mechanism worth comparing.
2. Select analogous contexts that share that mechanism.
3. Extract transferable patterns, not finished solutions.
4. State why each analogy may fail or overreach.
5. Convert useful patterns into opportunity questions.

## Expected Outcome

- Analogous contexts.
- Transferable patterns.
- Analogy limits.
- How Might We seeds rooted in evidence or accepted tensions.

## Provenance Requirements

Represent analogies as hypotheses unless supported by evidence. Connect each
pattern to the mechanism, constraint, or stakeholder need it addresses.
