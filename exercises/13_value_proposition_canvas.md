# 13. Value Proposition Canvas

## Attribution

Adapted for this agentic workflow from the value proposition canvas structure
used in the source materials. The broader exercise sequence is attributed to
IDEO.org Design Kit; the canvas framework is credited to Strategyzer.

## Purpose

Summarize stakeholder jobs, pains, and gains so later ideation can respond to
real needs without prematurely defining a solution.

## Inputs

- Stakeholder map
- Audience segments
- Interview, photojournal, power, and resource-flow claims
- Active challenge frame and impact goals

## Procedure

1. Select one customer or stakeholder segment at a time so different roles do
   not collapse into a single generic persona.
2. Capture that segment's jobs: what they are trying to accomplish functionally,
   socially, emotionally, or operationally.
3. Capture pains: obstacles, risks, frustrations, costs, unmet needs, and
   negative consequences in the current situation.
4. Capture gains: desired outcomes, benefits, progress, relief, and signs of
   success from that stakeholder's perspective.
5. Translate the evidence into value hypotheses by connecting possible products,
   services, gain creators, and pain relievers to validated jobs, pains, and
   gains. Keep those hypotheses as inputs for later ideation, not as final
   solutions.

## Tasks

1. Identify each stakeholder role that needs a persona-style map.
2. Extract jobs to be done, pains, gains, constraints, and motivations.
3. Separate validated human evidence from agentic inference.
4. Identify where stakeholder needs diverge or conflict.
5. Prepare value hypotheses as inputs for later ideation, not as final
   solutions.

## Expected Outcome

- Persona mapping for each relevant stakeholder.
- Jobs to be done.
- Pains and gains.
- Divergent needs across roles.
- Value hypotheses and validation gaps.

## Provenance Requirements

Each persona element must cite supporting claims. Deprecated or contradicted
claims cannot be used unless explicitly superseded by stronger later evidence.
