# 7. Extremes and Mainstreams

## Attribution

Adapted for this agentic workflow from the IDEO.org Design Kit method
"Extremes and Mainstreams."

## Purpose

Identify mainstream users and extreme users or contexts so the challenge frame
does not collapse around an average case.

## Inputs

- Audience hypotheses
- Power map
- Participation plan
- Existing human-input evidence

## Procedure

1. List the different people who might use, deliver, influence, or be affected
   by a future solution.
2. Identify meaningful spectrums in the audience, such as age, household size,
   access, expertise, geography, risk exposure, or intensity of need.
3. Select mainstream participants and people at the edges of those spectrums so
   the team learns from both typical and atypical experiences.
4. Ask extreme users how they currently handle similar situations, what works or
   fails for them, and how they might use a future solution.
5. Recruit sensitively and make participants who are often excluded feel welcome
   and respected.

## Tasks

1. Define mainstream stakeholder roles for the challenge.
2. Identify extreme users, edge contexts, or atypical cases.
3. Explain what each extreme case may reveal about needs, constraints, or
   failure modes.
4. Separate evidence-backed segments from recruiting hypotheses.
5. Recommend where further human evidence is needed.

## Expected Outcome

- Audience segments.
- Extreme and mainstream user definitions.
- Edge-case needs and risks.
- Recruiting or evidence-collection questions.

## Provenance Requirements

Link each segment to evidence or mark it as a hypothesis. Connect segment
claims to persona and interview outputs where available.
