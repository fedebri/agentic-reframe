# 7. Extremes and Mainstreams

## Purpose

Identify mainstream users and extreme users or contexts so the challenge frame
does not collapse around an average case.

## Inputs

- Audience hypotheses
- Power map
- Participation plan
- Existing human-input evidence

## Tasks

1. Define mainstream stakeholder roles for the challenge.
2. Identify extreme users, edge contexts, or atypical cases.
3. Explain what each extreme case may reveal about needs, constraints, or
   failure modes.
4. Separate evidence-backed segments from recruiting hypotheses.
5. Recommend where further human evidence is needed.

## Expected Outcome

- Audience segments.
- Extreme and mainstream user definitions.
- Edge-case needs and risks.
- Recruiting or evidence-collection questions.

## Provenance Requirements

Link each segment to evidence or mark it as a hypothesis. Connect segment
claims to persona and interview outputs where available.
