# 12. Project Plan

## Attribution

Adapted for this agentic workflow from the IDEO.org Design Kit method "Create a
Project Plan."

## Purpose

Build a practical project plan for the remaining discovery work and for handing
off into later ideation and prototyping phases, which remain out of scope.

## Inputs

- Active challenge frame
- Participation plan
- Research gaps
- Human-evidence needs
- Constraints, resource flows, and stakeholder risks

## Procedure

1. Start with a calendar and mark key dates, including deadlines, meetings,
   travel, fieldwork windows, partner availability, and team constraints.
2. Review budget and staffing against the work ahead, noting capability gaps,
   resource limits, and possible ways around constraints.
3. Identify what the team must learn before entering the field, including who to
   speak with and what to read.
4. Plan fieldwork logistics, including number of visits, partner involvement,
   participant access, and whether physical materials or prototypes will later
   need time, money, or people to produce.
5. Treat the plan as revisable, but make assumptions and dependencies explicit
   before the team starts executing.

## Tasks

1. Identify remaining discovery activities and dependencies.
2. Map timeline, staffing, budget questions, access needs, and fieldwork needs.
3. Convert evidence gaps into concrete research tasks.
4. Flag operational risks that could block later ideation or prototyping.
5. Produce a plan that can be revised as new evidence arrives.

## Expected Outcome

- Timeline and sequencing assumptions.
- Staffing and capability needs.
- Budget or resource questions.
- Fieldwork and human-input collection plan.
- Risks and review checkpoints.

## Provenance Requirements

Every project-plan task should trace back to an evidence gap, open question,
constraint, stakeholder need, or accepted tension.
