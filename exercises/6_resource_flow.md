# 6. Resource Flow

## Attribution

Adapted for this agentic workflow from the IDEO.org Design Kit method "Resource
Flow."

## Purpose

Map critical resource flows among stakeholders. Money is the default starting
point, but the model may also track time, labor, care, infrastructure,
attention, trust, data, and other scarce resources.

## Inputs

- Stakeholder map
- Human-input resource-flow maps in `human_input/resource_flow/`
- Interview notes, field observations, or brief evidence about resource use

## Procedure

1. Conduct the exercise during or after an interview with someone who can
   describe how resources enter and leave their household, organization, or
   stakeholder group.
2. Invite the participant to draw the map when possible; otherwise, draw it with
   them and confirm each item.
3. List or draw everything that brings resources in. Start with money, then
   include non-cash assets such as labor, seeds, livestock, care, data, trust, or
   infrastructure.
4. List or draw everything that takes resources out, including obligations or
   informal transfers that may not be described as payments.
5. Ask which inflows and outflows matter most, what is most expensive, what is
   essential, what is scarce, and how often each flow happens.
6. Add timing to the map when useful, because resources may arrive in bursts but
   need to cover recurring needs.

## Tasks

1. Identify incoming and outgoing resources for each relevant stakeholder.
2. Record resource type, frequency, dependency, and perceived value.
3. Identify bottlenecks, leakage, scarcity, and timing mismatches.
4. Distinguish observed flows from inferred flows.
5. Generate a resource-flow capture template if human evidence is absent.

## Expected Outcome

- Resource-flow map by stakeholder.
- Bottleneck and dependency claims.
- Timing or frequency notes.
- Missing-evidence template when needed.

## Provenance Requirements

Every flow must include source, resource type, direction, stakeholder endpoint,
confidence, and validation status. Inferred flows must remain hypotheses until
validated.
