# 11. Interviews

## Attribution

Adapted for this agentic workflow from the IDEO.org Design Kit methods
"Interview," "Expert Interview," and "Group Interview."

## Purpose

Analyze or generate interview materials for direct human evidence. Interviews
may be general participant interviews, expert interviews, or group interviews.

## Inputs

- Interview notes or transcripts in `human_input/interviews/`
- Active stakeholder, audience, power, and resource-flow claims
- Open questions generated by prior exercises

## Procedure

1. Choose the interview type that matches the evidence need: individual
   participant interviews for lived experience, expert interviews for context or
   system knowledge, and group interviews for community dynamics.
2. Keep individual participant interviews small, with no more than a few team
   members present and clear roles for interviewing, note-taking, and
   photography when appropriate.
3. Prepare questions in advance, beginning broadly with life, values, habits, or
   context before narrowing toward the challenge.
4. Capture direct statements carefully and separate them from interpretation;
   observe body language and setting as additional context, not as proof.
5. For expert interviews, recruit varied perspectives, preview the topic and
   time needed, ask informed questions, and stay flexible enough to follow
   unexpected lines of inquiry.
6. For group interviews, choose the group composition intentionally, meet in a
   neutral and accessible place, assign facilitation and note-taking roles, and
   actively include quieter participants.

## Tasks

1. Detect available general, expert, and group interview evidence.
2. Analyze present evidence for needs, motivations, constraints, quotes, and
   contradictions.
3. Separate direct statements from agent interpretation.
4. Generate interview templates when evidence is absent.
5. Route unresolved questions to the most suitable interview type.

## Expected Outcome

- Interview insights.
- Quote or note evidence references.
- Interview templates for missing evidence.
- Validation updates for prior claims.
- New open questions for human review.

## Provenance Requirements

Direct interview statements must cite transcript or note files. Interpretations
must be separate claims with lower confidence unless corroborated.
