# 3. Align Participatory Approach

## Purpose

Define how affected participants should be involved in the discovery process
and where participation, consent, representation, or power risks need attention.

## Inputs

- Framed challenge candidates
- Audience hypotheses
- Available human-input evidence
- Prior graph claims about stakeholders and power

## Tasks

1. Identify groups directly affected by the challenge.
2. Decide which groups should inform, review, co-create, or validate discovery
   outputs.
3. Flag participation barriers, consent needs, and representation risks.
4. Identify where human evidence is present and where templates are needed.
5. Propose a participation plan for the remaining exercises.

## Expected Outcome

- Participation plan by stakeholder role.
- Engagement risks and mitigation questions.
- Human-in-the-loop evidence needs.
- Template requests for interviews, photojournals, or resource-flow mapping
  where evidence is absent.

## Provenance Requirements

Create graph claims for participant roles, engagement assumptions, and power or
consent risks. Link template requests to missing-evidence events.
