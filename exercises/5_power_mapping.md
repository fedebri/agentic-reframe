# 5. Power Mapping

## Attribution

Adapted for this agentic workflow from the IDEO.org Design Kit method "Power
Mapping."

## Purpose

Map who has influence, decision rights, access, vulnerability, or dependency in
the challenge context.

## Inputs

- Audience hypotheses
- Participation plan
- Brief and supporting evidence
- Human-input notes about institutions, communities, or decision-makers

## Procedure

1. Decide whether to run the mapping digitally or in person, then prepare a
   shared space where stakeholders and power tokens can be placed visibly.
2. List community members, partners, designers, and other project stakeholders;
   capture their likely roles on the project.
3. Identify relevant power assets, such as money, decision authority, knowledge,
   connections, permission, communication influence, reputation, wellness,
   majority status, racial privilege, or context-specific assets.
4. Build a power inventory by mapping which stakeholders hold which assets and
   where some hold more or less power than others.
5. Reflect on opportunities to redistribute or share power, including decision
   rights, compensation, narrative authority, translation access, and practical
   project operations.

## Tasks

1. Identify stakeholders and stakeholder groups.
2. Map each stakeholder's influence, authority, dependency, and vulnerability.
3. Identify who can enable, block, benefit from, or be harmed by change.
4. Surface representation gaps and power asymmetries.
5. Flag tensions that must be preserved during synthesis.

## Expected Outcome

- Stakeholder power map.
- Power asymmetry notes.
- Governance or decision-right questions.
- Risks related to exclusion, tokenism, or misaligned incentives.

## Provenance Requirements

Represent stakeholder relationships as graph claims and edges. Preserve
unresolved power tensions rather than forcing consensus.
