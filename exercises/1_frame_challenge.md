# 1. Frame Challenge

## Attribution

Adapted for this agentic workflow from the IDEO.org Design Kit method "Frame
Your Design Challenge."

## Purpose

Define the challenge container for the rest of the sprint. The frame must name
who is affected and sit at the right altitude: specific enough to guide inquiry,
but broad enough to avoid smuggling in a solution.

## Inputs

- `docs/brief.md`
- Supporting documents assigned by the orchestrator
- Prior graph claims, if this exercise is rerun

## Procedure

1. Draft the challenge as a short, memorable question or sentence that states
   the problem without prescribing the answer.
2. State the key outcome the project is trying to achieve and connect it to the
   broader impact goal.
3. Capture context and constraints that shape the frame, including ecosystem
   shifts, technology limits, geography, timing, access, and institutional
   factors.
4. Generate several possible solution directions as a stress test. If the frame
   cannot produce multiple plausible paths, it is probably too narrow.
5. Revise the challenge question using the outcome, constraints, and solution
   stress test. Keep rejected frames with reasons for rejection.

## Tasks

1. Extract the stated challenge, design clue, problem, affected audience, and
   implied goal from the brief.
2. Identify what is known, assumed, missing, or already solution-shaped.
3. Draft multiple challenge frames, including at least one How Might We form.
4. Test each frame for audience clarity, scope, altitude, and solution bias.
5. Select the strongest candidate and document rejected alternatives.

## Expected Outcome

- A framed challenge candidate with a named audience.
- A short rationale for why the frame is neither too broad nor too narrow.
- A list of altitude risks and hidden assumptions.
- Open questions that would materially improve framing confidence.

## Provenance Requirements

Create graph claims for the selected frame, audience assumptions, rejected
frames, and altitude risks. Link each claim to the brief or supporting evidence.
